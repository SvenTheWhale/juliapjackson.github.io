Methodology

A Structured, Standards‑Aligned Approach

What is the problem that we are trying to solve? This question anchors my methodology. I begin by understanding the state of the state—how the work is currently functioning, what outcomes the business needs, what risks or constraints exist, and what assumptions may be shaping decisions. This grounding allows me to clarify the real problem, define scope, and establish the requirements that will guide all downstream work.

My approach blends business systems analysis with business continuity management and governance. It is anchored in ISO‑22301, ISO‑22318, NIST guidance, and BABOK. I translate these standards into clear, practical expectations that teams can use. The goal is always the same: documentation and decisions that are accurate, repeatable, and aligned with both governance requirements and real operational flow.

Clarifying Requirements, Needs, Risks, and Scope

I start by assessing the current state and understanding what the business is trying to accomplish. This includes business needs, desired outcomes, regulatory or governance requirements, known risks, constraints, dependencies, and assumptions that need validation. This ensures that all subsequent work is grounded in a shared understanding of the real problem.

Understanding the Operational Reality

Once the problem space is defined, I work to understand how the business actually operates. I identify critical steps, dependencies, data flows, and decision points, validating these details with SMEs to ensure documentation reflects real practice, not inherited assumptions.

Mapping Processes, Data, and System Behavior

With requirements and operational context established, I map processes, data flows, and system behavior to identify gaps, risks, and opportunities. Mapping becomes a tool—not the starting point—to ensure that solutions and strategies reflect how work actually moves through the organization.

Structured Documentation and Standards Alignment

I create documentation that is consistent, traceable, and aligned with standards. This includes business process documentation, continuity strategies, recovery expectations, data mapping, system behavior analysis, and governance requirements. I align these materials with ISO‑22301, ISO‑22318, and NIST expectations.

Cross‑Functional Facilitation

I bring together functional teams, IT, compliance, and operations to ensure shared understanding. I facilitate working sessions that surface gaps, dependencies, and risks early, reducing rework and strengthening decision‑making.

Agile Delivery and Iterative Improvement

I manage Agile delivery within my workstreams, including backlog refinement, sprint planning, and coordination with product owners and technical leads. I support Scrum ceremonies and maintain clear documentation so teams stay aligned and unblocked.

Risk‑Aware Decision Support

I help teams understand the operational, regulatory, and customer impacts of decisions. I identify where established practices no longer meet expectations and offer practical, flexible improvements that enhance clarity, resilience, and audit credibility.

Application Familiarity

I work comfortably across the tools that support business systems analysis, business continuity management and governance, and cross-functional delivery. My experience includes Visio for process and workflow mapping, Confluence and SharePoint for structured documentation, Jira for Agile work at Cargill, Excel for analysis, Teams for collaboration, and GitHub for managing and publishing my professional portfolio site.
