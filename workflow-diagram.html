Business Continuity Workflow

This workflow reflects the structured approach I use to lead Business Impact Analysis (BIA), scenario and dependency mapping, continuity strategy development, Business Continuity Strategy Documentation and Runbook creation across complex operational environments.

BIA Part 1: Identification of Critical Capabilities

  • Define scope, objectives and assumptions
  • Identify applicable laws, including Federal, State, Municipal
  • Identify first-round leaders and SMEs
  • Review corporate capability / process list with SMEs
  • Remove irrelevant capabilities
  • Identify next-level SMEs for deeper discovery
  • Conduct capability evaluation sessions
  • Determine capability tiers (1–5)
  • Filter to Tier 1–3 capabilities
  • Validate Tier 1–3 list with leaders and SMEs
  • Identify SMEs for dependency discovery

BIA Part 2: Identification of Dependencies

  • Group capabilities logically (EHS, FSQR, Plant Ops, Receiving, Shipping)
  • Conduct dependency sessions with SMEs
  • Capture applications, technologies, equipment, and third parties
  • Capture roles responsible for decision-making
  • Capture application RTOs
  • Determine application criticality
  • Move capabilities to continuity strategy development

Continuity Strategy & Runbook Development

  • Prioritize most critical capabilities (Tier 0 and Tier 1 applications)
  • Conduct plant SME sessions
  • Identify continuity steps for dependency outages
  • Identify high-level issues and refine details
  • Document strategies in standardized format (TGRC BCP → Runbook v1 → Runbook v2)
  • Shift to process-based continuity
  • Refine content with SMEs and leadership
  • Send draft for SME review and approval
  • Conduct follow-up sessions as needed
  • Publish approved strategies to production
  • Notify stakeholders of availability

Exercises & Scenario Validation

  • Design severe-but-plausible scenarios
  • Conduct tabletop exercises
  • Train leaders and SMEs
  • Integrate lessons learned into strategies

Continuous Improvement

  • Narrow capabilities and refine dependencies
  • Balance depth and usability of strategies
  • Collaborate with experienced SMEs to refine content
  • Evolve toward iterative, application-focused continuity
