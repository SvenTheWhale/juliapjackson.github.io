Cargill — The Runbook

A new customer contract required CPNA to produce a formal business continuity plan, but the business had no completed continuity work and no dedicated resource to manage the lifecycle. I was brought in to meet the immediate requirement and was later moved into the business to lead continuity across CPNA.

TGRC owned the enterprise BCM framework, but it didn’t align with how protein manufacturing operates. CPNA needed a continuity approach that matched real operational flow, met customer and audit expectations, and could scale across 46 plants and 9 distribution centers. The Fusion-based approach generated strategies that were not usable during an event, and the business needed something practical, standards-aligned, and operationally intuitive.

I built a new continuity framework—the Runbook—designed to meet corporate requirements, ISO/NIST standards, and CPNA’s operational reality. I replaced the Fusion structure with a format that reflected how work actually moves through a protein facility. I defined requirements using my business systems analyst background, researched industry standards for structuring continuity strategies, and created multiple prototypes before selecting Confluence as the platform that could support hierarchy, cross-linking, and fast consumption during an incident.

I partnered with SMEs across operations, FSQR, supply chain, IT, HR, and procurement to validate critical processes, dependencies, and recovery expectations. I established governance, templates, and documentation standards, and delivered training across CPNA and global functions. I also managed ongoing updates, releases, and continuous improvement as the Runbook became the foundation for continuity work across the business.

The result was a practical, scalable continuity framework that aligned with corporate expectations while fitting the operational realities of CPNA. It improved audit readiness, strengthened decision-making during incidents, and provided a clear, consistent structure for continuity work across all sites.

Cargill — Customer Audits

CPNA faced increasing customer audit demands, each requiring continuity evidence that aligned with ISO, NIST, and corporate expectations. Existing responses were inconsistent and difficult to trace back to standards, and operational teams were spending significant time trying to interpret what customers were actually asking for. Leadership needed a structured, repeatable way to answer continuity-related audit questions with accuracy and confidence.

I reviewed customer audit requirements across multiple global partners and mapped them to corporate BCM expectations, ISO 22301, ISO 22318, NIST guidance, and CPNA’s operational practices. Because I had already built the Runbook, I had a complete set of continuity documentation that reflected real operational flow, critical dependencies, and recovery expectations. I also had access to global audit responses and the corporate policies and procedures covering data management, backup, cybersecurity, training, and related controls. With these sources, I was able to analyze gaps, reconcile inconsistencies, and build a structured response model without needing to rely heavily on others for content.

I validated alignment with TGRC and corporate BCM to ensure accuracy against enterprise standards, but the core work—analysis, mapping, structuring, and drafting—was done directly by me. The result was a single, authoritative set of continuity-related audit responses that tied each customer question back to the appropriate standard, requirement, or operational practice.

This framework improved accuracy, reduced rework, and strengthened CPNA’s credibility with major customers. It also reduced the burden on operational teams by giving them clear, validated language that reflected how the business actually operated. The model became the foundation for future customer audits and supported CPNA’s broader continuity, compliance, and customer-facing commitments.

Securian — Regulatory & Cross-Border

Securian was expanding into a new regulatory jurisdiction that required strict alignment across business processes, data handling, system behavior, and cross-border controls. The Claims effort was organized into three major workstreams. I led one of those teams, responsible for the requirements, data, and operational impacts tied to our portion of the expansion.

I partnered closely with functional teams and IT to define what the regulatory expansion required and how it needed to operate across systems and processes. I facilitated working sessions to surface process differences, regulatory constraints, and system impacts, and translated those into clear, testable requirements for my workstream.

I managed the Agile delivery for my team, including backlog refinement, sprint planning, and coordinating with product owners and technical leads to ensure work was sequenced correctly. I aligned dependencies across the other Claims workstreams, maintained transparency, and ensured cross-team impacts were understood early. I supported Scrum ceremonies and provided clear documentation to keep the team focused and unblocked.

A significant part of the effort involved mapping data across systems to ensure accuracy, completeness, and compliance with cross-border rules. I analyzed source-to-target mappings, identified gaps and inconsistencies, and worked with data and technology partners to resolve issues before they reached testing. I also ensured that downstream impacts—reporting, customer communications, regulatory submissions, and operational workflows—were fully understood and accounted for within my scope.

Throughout the project, I supported risk-aware decision-making by clarifying dependencies, surfacing operational impacts, and ensuring that compliance requirements were reflected in both business processes and system behavior. I worked closely with QA and UAT teams to validate that requirements were implemented correctly and that the end-to-end flow met regulatory expectations for the areas under my responsibility.

The result was a compliant, well-structured expansion within my workstream that aligned business, technology, and regulatory needs. The work strengthened Securian’s readiness for future cross-border initiatives and improved clarity across teams by establishing a shared understanding of requirements, data, and operational impacts.

Green Tree / Ditech — Enterprise Loan Migration to an Industry-Standard Platform

Green Tree was migrating 1.5 million home-loan records from a proprietary servicing system to an industry-standard platform. The migration required precise data mapping, regulatory alignment, and a clear understanding of how the new system would behave once the data was moved. The work touched high-risk operational areas including collections, foreclosure, bankruptcy, customer communications, and regulatory reporting. Leadership needed confidence that the migration would preserve accuracy, compliance, and operational continuity.

I supported the enterprise migration by analyzing business processes, data structures, and system behavior across both the legacy and target platforms. I reviewed source data, validated mappings, and identified gaps that could create downstream issues in servicing, reporting, investor requirements, or regulatory compliance. I worked with business and technology teams to resolve discrepancies and ensure that the mapping reflected real operational needs and regulatory obligations.

I also analyzed how the new system handled key servicing functions—payment processing, escrow, investor reporting, customer notices, and regulatory triggers—to ensure that migrated data would behave correctly once in production. This required close coordination with SMEs, QA teams, and compliance partners to validate that the system’s logic aligned with federal and state servicing requirements.

Throughout the migration, I documented processes, clarified dependencies, and supported testing to ensure that the data and system behavior matched expectations. I helped teams understand how changes in data structure would affect operations and ensured that regulatory requirements were preserved throughout the transition.

The migration delivered accurate, compliant data into the new platform and supported a smooth transition for both internal teams and customers. The work strengthened operational reliability and reduced risk by ensuring that the new system reflected real servicing needs and regulatory obligations.
